Selected case studies

Copy that converts the unconvertible.

Each case below involved a brand struggling to reach a skeptical, midlife audience. Here's what changed.

01

Vitality & Co.

From 'another supplement brand' to trusted wellness partner

↑ 142% conversions

The Challenge

A DTC supplement brand targeting women 45+ was blending in with thousands of competitors. Their messaging was clinical and impersonal — exactly the kind of language this audience ignores. Midlife women research before they buy, and the brand's copy wasn't passing the credibility test.

The Solution

I repositioned the brand around lived experience rather than science claims. Instead of 'clinically proven formulas,' we led with 'For the body you're in now.' Long-form landing pages addressed the specific health anxieties of perimenopausal and post-menopausal women with warmth, candor, and zero condescension.

02

Halcyon Wealth

Making retirement planning feel less like a root canal

↑ 3.1× demo requests

The Challenge

A financial advisory firm wanted to attract pre-retirees (55-65) but was hemorrhaging leads. Their copy was stiff, jargon-heavy, and read like a compliance document. This audience — skeptical of financial services after 2008 — bounced within seconds.

The Solution

I used the 'skeptic's framework' — leading with the objection rather than hiding from it. Headlines like 'Yes, we charge fees. Here's exactly why that's better for you.' I humanized the advisors with narrative bios that told stories instead of listing credentials. Email sequences were rewritten to feel like advice from a trusted friend rather than a sales funnel.

03

Lark & Loom

Turning 'I'm too old for this brand' into 'This brand gets me'

↑ 89% repeat purchases

The Challenge

A home goods brand had beautiful products but was accidentally repelling its ideal customer — women 50+ — through copy that read like it was written for a 28-year-old. Words like 'obsessed,' 'game-changer,' and 'I'm screaming' made the actual buyer feel invisible.

The Solution

We didn't age the brand down or up — we widened the lens. Campaign copy and product stories started featuring multigenerational narratives. The tone shifted from hype to heritage. Email welcome sequences were rewritten to feel like a letter from a friend who has great taste, not a brand trying to be cool.

04

Peregrine Travel

Selling adventure to people who've heard every pitch

↑ 217% booked trips

The Challenge

A boutique travel company specializing in trips for 50+ travelers was struggling with a scattershot message. Their copy swung between 'bucket list' clichés and overly formal descriptions that bored the very people who had the time and money to travel.

The Solution

I developed a voice that balanced aspiration with practicality — because this audience wants to climb Machu Picchu but also wants to know about the bathroom situation. Trip descriptions now lead with the emotional payoff and follow with transparent logistics. Testimonials were woven into the copy itself, not buried on a separate page.

The approach

Trust is earned in the margins.

Skeptical audiences don't respond to urgency, scarcity, or social proof. They respond to resonance — the quiet feeling of being understood.

01

Lead with the objection

Before you convince, you must acknowledge. I open with the thing they're already thinking — the doubt, the suspicion, the 'yeah, but.' When you name the elephant, you earn the right to speak.

02

Write for the 2 a.m. Google

Midlifers don't impulse-buy at 10 a.m. They research at midnight, comparing options in a dozen tabs. My copy meets them there — with depth, candor, and the patience to answer questions they haven't yet formed.

03

Never condescend

This audience has been marketed to for decades. They can smell a script from a mile away. I write with the assumption that the reader is at least as smart as I am — because they usually are.

04

Clarity over cleverness

The skeptical mind is scanning for bullshit. A pun that would delight a 22-year-old triggers a 55-year-old's defenses. I choose precision over wit, warmth over flash, and honesty over hype — every time.

05

The long game beats the quick win

Conversion isn't a moment — it's a relationship built across six touchpoints. I structure copy ecosystems so each piece does its job without the reader feeling 'funneled.'

06

Respect the lived experience

Midlife consumers bring decades of experience to every decision. They've been burned, delighted, disappointed, and surprised. My copy honors that history instead of pretending it doesn't exist.

About Jane

I write copy for the people marketers forgot.

Twelve years ago, I was a brand manager at a DTC company watching our midlife customers bounce — not because the product was wrong, but because the words were. The copy was written by 26-year-olds for 26-year-olds, and our actual customer (48, skeptical, savvy) felt like she was reading a language she didn't speak.

That was the beginning of everything. I started studying what makes skeptical audiences lean in instead of click away. I read the research on persuasion resistance. I conducted hundreds of customer interviews with people who'd been marketed to for 40+ years and had developed what I now call "the immune response" — the ability to detect and dismiss marketing in under three seconds.

Today, I work with brands who are serious about earning trust from the most discerning audiences. My clients range from supplement startups to financial advisors to travel companies — united by a shared problem: they're selling to people who've seen it all.

I'm based in Portland, Oregon. I work with a small, curated roster of clients at a time. If that sounds like the kind of collaboration you're looking for, I'd love to hear from you.
